# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Coordinators

### Role Summary
Release Coordinators oversee release readiness and execution across teams. They ensure milestones are met, deployment activities are tracked, and timelines are communicated clearly to stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the release schedule and milestone plan
- Coordinate go/no-go readiness checks with engineering, QA, and operations
- Maintain and drive completion of deployment checklists
- Track release risks, dependencies, and escalation paths
- Communicate release timelines, status, and changes to stakeholders

### Goals
- Deliver predictable, low-risk releases
- Reduce release delays caused by coordination gaps
- Improve release transparency and stakeholder confidence

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness updates and go/no-go summaries
- Deployment timeline announcements and change notifications
- Cross-functional coordination in release channels and planning meetings

---

## QA Leads

### Role Summary
QA Leads define and steward the testing strategy for the project. They establish quality metrics, promote testing best practices, and ensure quality activities are consistently applied across teams.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy across unit, integration, end-to-end, and regression levels
- Establish quality gates and metrics for release readiness
- Ensure test plans and acceptance criteria are complete and testable
- Review defect trends and lead quality improvement actions
- Coach teams on test design, automation, and risk-based testing

### Goals
- Increase defect detection earlier in the development lifecycle
- Improve product stability and release confidence
- Maintain consistent, measurable quality standards

### Typical Communication
- Test strategy briefings and quality dashboard updates
- Defect triage facilitation and root-cause discussions
- Guidance in planning sessions and release readiness reviews

---

## Stakeholder Liaisons

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Liaisons act as the central communication bridge between delivery teams and stakeholder groups. They gather feedback, clarify expectations, and ensure concerns are routed to the right owners.

### Responsibilities
- Identify key stakeholder groups and maintain contact channels
- Collect, synthesize, and prioritize stakeholder feedback
- Ensure stakeholder concerns are documented and tracked to resolution
- Coordinate messaging on scope, timelines, risks, and decisions
- Support alignment during key milestones and change events

### Goals
- Improve trust through consistent and transparent communication
- Reduce misalignment between stakeholders and delivery teams
- Ensure stakeholder feedback informs planning and execution decisions

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder briefings and feedback summaries
- Decision logs and follow-up action tracking
- Coordination with product and project leads on status messaging

---

## Automation Champions

### Role Summary
Automation Champions identify and implement opportunities to automate repetitive project and delivery processes. They assess workflow bottlenecks and advocate for tooling that improves speed, quality, and reliability.

### Responsibilities
- Map and review recurring workflow bottlenecks and manual steps
- Propose and implement process automation improvements
- Advocate for practical tooling adoption with measurable outcomes
- Partner with teams to standardize automated workflows and templates
- Monitor automation impact and continuously refine processes

### Goals
- Reduce manual effort and process cycle time
- Increase consistency in project execution and reporting
- Improve team efficiency through sustainable automation practices

### Typical Communication
- Workflow improvement proposals and impact reports
- Tooling demos and enablement sessions
- Regular updates on automation backlog and outcomes
